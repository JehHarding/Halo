Cookie Policy

Last updated: 23 June 2026

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, which cookies the Halo website (haloapp.co.uk) uses, and how you can manage them. It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences and understand how you use it. Some cookies are essential for the site to work; others are optional and used to improve your experience or help us understand our audience.

2. Cookies we use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
halo_cookie_consent Essential Remembers whether you have accepted or declined non-essential cookies, so we do not ask again on every visit. 1 year
Analytics cookies Analytics (optional) Help us understand how visitors use the site, which pages are most visited, and where traffic comes from. No personal data is shared with third parties for advertising. Up to 2 years

We do not use advertising or tracking cookies, and we do not share data with third-party advertisers.

3. Essential cookies

The cookie consent cookie (halo_cookie_consent) is strictly necessary for the site to function correctly with regard to your preferences. It does not require your consent to be set, as it exists solely to honour your choice.

4. Optional cookies

Analytics cookies are only set if you click "Accept" on our cookie banner. If you decline, only the essential consent cookie is stored. You can change your preference at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site.

5. Managing cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect how the site works. Here are links to cookie management guides for the main browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Safari
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge

6. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy if we change the cookies we use. The date at the top of this page will always reflect the latest version.
