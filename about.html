Our Story

Where Halo
began.

A pet who defied every prognosis. A care routine no single app could hold. And the decision to build something better.

01
How it began

One week old. Already impossible to leave behind.

A litter of kittens. The plan was to choose one. What happened instead was that two came home, inseparable even at that age.

But within weeks, messages began arriving with a weight to them. Angel's breathing was becoming laboured. The vet bills were mounting beyond what the family could manage. They asked whether she would still be taken, and if not, there was no other option for her.

"The decision was already made. I had already chosen to have her in my life."

Angel came home at two months old. Small, fragile, and entirely determined.

02
The diagnosis

Three heart conditions. No cure. A £3,000 scan that changed everything.

What followed was a procession of specialists and a cardiac scan that confirmed what had been feared. Angel carries three simultaneous heart conditions, and one of them, paradoxically, is the thing keeping her alive. To operate would be to disturb the balance entirely.

The only path forward: hold her steady. Three medications, daily. Blood tests every three months. An annual cardiac scan. A level of care that demands precision, and a level of love that makes it feel effortless.

"Two weeks, two months, or two years." That was the only answer the cardiologist could give.

Angel is approaching her seventh birthday.

"Two weeks, two months, or two years."
The cardiologist's prognosis, six and a half years ago.
Why we built Halo

When one missed dose is unthinkable, you need somewhere to hold it all.

Managing Angel's care meant carrying an extraordinary amount of detail. Three medications with precise daily windows. The cardiologist's name and number. The date of the next blood test. The annual scan appointment. The dietary notes. The emergency contact list that any carer would need.

The anxiety was sharpest around travel. Who would care for her? Would they remember everything? What if one dose was skipped, even once? Plans were quietly cancelled. The mental spreadsheet never really rested.

"If you have everything in one place, all the records, all the contacts, all the routines, you can give your pet another chance."

No app existed that could hold all of it, beautifully. So we built Halo. One calm, elegant place for everything your pet's health requires, so that whoever is caring for them has exactly what they need, and you can finally breathe.

Halo is named for Angel. Quietly, and with everything that name means.

Angel, seven years in, living fully.

Six years, captured.
